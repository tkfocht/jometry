Statistics

Att: Attempts

Number of attempts to ring in, as given in the box score.

BuzC: Correct on Buzz

Correct answers on buzz. Equivalent to COR from the box score, minus any correct Daily Doubles.

Buz$: Score on Buzz

Score using only buzz-in responses. Equivalent to actual score and to Coryat score, except it ignores Daily Doubles entirely. Why not just use Coryat? The earliest version of this site was based soley on the box scores, and Coryat is not derivable from them. After that, I mostly just decided I liked Buz$ as an in-game contestant statistic better, as it only covers clues available to all players. I do believe Coryat is better for comparing performance at home, though.

Contention

An estimate of the probability that any individual correct response (for any contestant) blocked an opponent from buzzing in.

DDF: Daily Doubles Found

Daily Doubles found by the player.

DD+: Daily Doubles Over Expectation

An estimate of how many more Daily Doubles a player found versus an expectation that each correct response in a round is equally likely to lead to finding a Daily Double. This statistic is zero-sum: all positive DD+ must be balanced by negative DD+ for another contestant.

Solo: Buzzes that are uncontested

An estimate of how many correct responses did not block an opponent's attempt at a correct response. That is, it is an estimate of how many clues the contestant alone was correct on.

Timing: Buzzes created through buzzer timing

An estimate of how many additional response attempts a player gains through good timing, or gains through opponents' lack of timing. This statistic is almost zero-sum: most positive Timing must be balanced by negative Timing for another contestant, but attempts that are blocked by incorrect responses and then not retried on the rebound keep this from balancing exactly.

This site is not affiliated with Jeopardy!. Data is taken from their published box scores and from J! Archive.
